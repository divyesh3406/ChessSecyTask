More Than a Game: Inside the Chess Club

At an institute widely renowned for its intense and demanding academic structure, the brightest minds still find time to gather around the 64 squares and dive into hours of strategic psychological warfare.

Follow the club on social media

Forging the Competitive Spirit

The Chess Club IIT Kanpur has long been a haven for the campus’s chess players, whether they are seasoned veterans or newcomers. It doesn't matter if you’re a Grandmaster or if you are completely unsure how en passant works—everyone has a seat at our tables.

Over the years, the club has evolved from a small but passionate group of casual enthusiasts into a major competitive hub in the region, all while maintaining the feel of an extended family.

Our Annual Tournaments

To promote competitive drive and participation, the club hosts multiple events throughout the academic calendar:

League of Legends

An annual team-based tournament. The absolute best part? Not only do current students participate, but battle-hardened IITK alumni return to test their tactical edge against the new generation.

Fresher’s Chess League

A team-based league strictly made for the freshers, introducing first-year students to the high-stakes thrill of dynamic player auctions and fast, intense matches.

IITK Speed Chess Championship

Mimicking the Speed Chess Championship format, this event is held over late, cold December nights. It closely follows standard blitz and bullet configurations of the SCC, punishing even minor slips of time management.

The Historic Breakthrough

February 2026 Milestone

In early 2026, the club shattered its own ceilings. We hosted our first-ever official FIDE-Rated tournament: The SBI GIC Ltd. presents FIDE Rated Open Rapid Chess Tournament 2026.

Spanning 9 gruelling Swiss rounds with a substantial prize pool of ₹2 Lakh, the event gathered 336 participants—including International Masters, FIDE Masters, and titled competitors. While IM Anuj Shrivatri ultimately took home the trophy, the real success belonged to our student management team, executing an event of this scale for the first time in history.

The Real Unseen Moves

What does it actually take to run an ecosystem like this? Behind the clean graphic posters and smooth tournament logistics lies the late-night reality: constant negotiations with the DoSA office for venue permissions, pondering over font selections, setting up boards, and occasionally facing the heartbreak of scheduling changes after weeks of preparation and planning.

Yet, that shared struggle is precisely what builds our unique culture. This is a place where blundering nine queens in a single game is completely fine, but missing out on the community, the family, and the experience is the real blunder in life.
